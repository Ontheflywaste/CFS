Wheelchair & Mobility Assistance

Getting through an airport is hard enough. For travelers with reduced mobility, it can be the hardest part of the whole trip. We make it calm, patient, and respectful — every single time.

Our promise

Patient people. Careful hands. Clear communication.

Our attendants treat every passenger like family. Training isn't a checkbox — it's a quarterly standard, reinforced in the field and tied to how agents are scheduled and promoted.

  • Curb‑to‑gate, gate‑to‑gate, and gate‑to‑curb coverage
  • ACAA‑compliant training + annual recertification
  • Electric and standard wheelchair fleet, inspected daily
  • Family and companion coordination built into every booking
What's covered

The whole journey through the airport.

Curb & greet

Meet at your drop‑off. Help with bags, check‑in, and security — at your pace, not anyone else's.

Terminal transit

Long walks and connections handled smoothly — including between‑terminal shuttles.

Gate & boarding

Priority boarding coordination and hand‑off to the flight crew for in‑seat assistance.

Arrival assistance

Met at the jet bridge, helped to baggage claim, and escorted to your ride.

Premium cabin standards

Ideal for first‑class and business passengers who expect a hotel‑style greeting.

Unaccompanied minors

Airlines choose Casual Flight Services for assisted minor programs — calm, safe, always documented.

Training

Four weeks of training. Quarterly refreshers. No shortcuts.

Every attendant completes hands‑on certification in safe transfers, equipment handling, passenger communication, and ACAA compliance — before their first shift alone.

  • Nationally standardized curriculum
  • Background checks + drug screening at hire
  • Quarterly refreshers on passenger communication
FAQ

Common questions.

How do I request assistance for a family member?

Book through our portal or ask your airline — most major carriers route wheelchair requests to Casual Flight Services automatically in supported airports.

Is there a charge to the passenger?

No. Wheelchair assistance at the airport is a free service required under the Air Carrier Access Act. Casual Flight Services is paid by the airline.

Can I travel with my own wheelchair?

Yes. We'll check it through as mobility equipment and provide a loaner during your time in the terminal.

Can a companion ride along?

Absolutely. We coordinate pace and path with companions so the whole party stays together through the airport.

Get in touch

Assistance, arranged with care.

For passengers: book through your airline or reach out and we'll coordinate. For airlines & airports: we're actively taking on new stations.
